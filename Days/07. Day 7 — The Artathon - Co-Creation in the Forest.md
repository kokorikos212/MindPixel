On this day we have committed to prepare discuss and perform our own activities. One of our advantages was that we were surrounded by the beautiful Pirin National Park which gave us a lot of inspiration and peace to grow on. In Small teams of 4-5 people, all of us from different countries, which let us to share different perspectives, backgrounds and so on. Everyone should be included and heard, participants shared ideas which lead them to make combined workshops, with one aim, but still diversified. So then it begins: with a peaceful walk to the nearby forest with a lot of hopes.  

The main part of the day we were creating ‘’Artathon”, also at the end we gave feedback to all teams.

But what exactly ‘’Artathon’’ means?

 Artathon is basically a creative challenge. Think of it as a mix between a workshop and a team creativity sprint. It’s structured session where participants work in teams to design a complete activity that fits the project’s theme.

During the Artathon we were dvided into 4 teams and we were asked to create an activity that could be used in the Erasmus + project. 

The teams were divided by their skills and each of us had one well-experienced participant in the field of youth work. 

Each group had approximately 2 hours to prepare their activities. We were equipped with laptops and different kind of materials which helped we to write down, create and present their ideas. 

We decided to conduct the activities in the forest, which helped us to relax.

Two of workshops were group tasks and two other individual tasks, the first two were about digital privacy and AI data processing, the second about getting in touch with our image. We gathered data about ourselves, processed them and guessed about different facts on different people. The following 2 groups focused on using meditation and general reconnecting with nature. 

After completing the activities, our group came together for an open discussion  where everyone shared their impressions and observations. We talked about what stood out to us, highlighting the creativity, preparation and strong teamwork each group demonstrated. It was clear that the participants had invested real effort into their tasks. At the same time we addressed several areas where improvement was possible, offering constructive feedback meant to support their growth rather than criticize.  Overall, the session felt meaningful because it encouraged both celebration of strengths and thoughtful self- development.
