Soundscape for Anxiety: (10-11:30)

We started by walking to the forest, gathering to get introduced to the activity (25 mins), and were invited to listen and collect (record) sounds from the forest while interacting with the natural objects (small groups of 2-4). We were also encouraged to add our own poems from previous sessions. (25 mins)

We went back to the benches and the group to start guided meditation and created a group soundscape by each creating a sound that fits the whole symphony. We started with simple, easier sounds (e.g., claps), then got the prompt to involve our own voices, and gradually got louder and louder (wierder and wierder, too! Like a chaotic symphony). At the later stages, we were brought back gradually to our initial volume and simpler sounds. (20mins)

  

Creating our own Soundscapes: (12-13:30)

This time was allocated for each team to work on their soundscape; we were encouraged to install (Audacity program) on our laptops and use it to combine the sounds and layer them so they would form a coherent soundscape. (90 mins)

  
  
  

Controversies Fishbowl: (15:30 - 17)

In this activity, we sat in two circles of chairs: the inner circle consisted of 4 chairs (one must always be empty), and the rest of the participants sat in the outer circle.

Participants are only allowed to talk if they were seated in the inner circle; meaning only 3 people would be able to debate at once. 

  

The facilitator read statements to discuss, and here are some examples:

1- Screen time is inherently bad.

2- Only adults should be allowed to use social media.

3- Social media is making people lonelier.

4- AI is toxic for creativity

5- Art done with technology doesn’t have the health benefits of the analogue art.

6- Youth workers should restrict the use of phones of the youth they work with.

  

Algorithmic Alchemy: (17:30-19)

  

In this session, we split into 5 teams (3 or 4 people), and the task was to use poems that we’d already written (highly poetic with many metaphors), read, or found online and feed them to some AI agents. 

First, with no explanation, just fed the poem into the AI agent and asked it to generate a picture about it.

Second, we started modifying the prompt and directing the work of the AI agent until the picture got closer to describing the emotion and words of the poems.

Lastly, we shared our results and viewed others’, and we tried to guess the prompting methods and the feelings and metaphors of the poems behind each picture.

  

The highlight of the day:

We particularly enjoyed the soundscape activity where we got to create our own soundtracks. Because it’s a creative process within nature, it allowed us to connect more with ourselves and the tools we’ve used to create such sounds. During this activity, we explored the natural sounds in the forest and interacted with them; However, it allowed us to connect as well and trigger each other’s creativity in a very constructive manner.
