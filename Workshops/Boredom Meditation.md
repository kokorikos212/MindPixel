# Boredom Meditation

**About:** Participants were encouraged to explore boredom and afterwards use their feelings or thoughts to express themselves creatively.

**What happened:** The active part lasted 45 minutes. Phones away, silence, no leaving the room, no disturbing others. The results varied: creative writing, drawing, doodling, performance art, and getting in touch with body or emotions.

> [!quote] [[Kamilla]]
> Turning away from participants, I fixed my eye on a white wall and found it comfortable. It was cleaning me, my mind of the emotional garbage I got from others, as the first tear fell, and then another, I lost control. I was surrounded, caught between the fear of staying and the dread of leaving. So I became statue. Yet, through it all, I wasn't bored for a moment. I'm never bored with living.

> [!quote] [[Aigly]]
> I am surprised how everyone let themselves be free once they leaned into it and accepted it.

> [!quote] [[Luna]]
> The boredom activity brought a lot to my awareness about my own fidgeting reactions and about how other people work with their boredom state. It showed me how we can use boredom as a starter into creative activities, but also how different we all are in how we manage our energy and creativity.

> [!quote] [[Nader]]
> The boredom session was the most impactful. I liked observing people being bored and how some of them allowed themselves to sit with it and others didn't allow one minute of listening to themselves. For me, I started to think that I have a decent tolerance for boredom. Maybe I can integrate it in little daily sessions.
