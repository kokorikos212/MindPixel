# 🎯 Topic Connection

This activity explores **how technology shapes identity, self-expression, and mental wellbeing**.

Instead of presenting ourselves through carefully curated social media profiles, participants anonymously share meaningful facts about themselves using **creative writing as an artistic form of self-expression**. The activity encourages genuine human interaction, challenges digital assumptions, and demonstrates that authentic connections are built through real conversations rather than online personas.

---

# 🧰 Materials Needed

- Small pieces of paper or index cards
- Pens or pencils
- A box or container
- Score sheet *(optional)*

---

# 📖 Activity Description

### Step 1 – Anonymous Self-Expression *(5 minutes)*

Each participant writes **3–5 facts** about themselves on a piece of paper **without writing their name**.

The facts should focus on things such as:

- Personality
- Interests
- Experiences
- Values
- Dreams
- Habits

Participants are encouraged **not** to describe obvious physical characteristics. Instead, they should write things that represent who they truly are beyond what people normally see through social media or technology.

---

### Step 2 – Collect the Papers

Once everyone has finished:

- Fold each paper.
- Place all papers into a box or container.
- Shuffle them thoroughly.

---

### Step 3 – Team Guessing *(15 minutes)*

Divide participants into teams of **3–6 people**.

Each team draws several anonymous fact sheets (for example, six).

For every sheet, the team discusses:

> **Who do we think wrote this?**

Each correct answer earns **1 point**.

**Special rule**

If a team accidentally draws the fact sheet of one of its own members:

- Do **not** guess it.
- Quietly return it to the box.
- Draw another sheet instead.

This encourages participants to interact with people outside their immediate friend group while keeping the activity fair.

---

### Step 4 – Reveal

When all teams have finished:

- Reveal the correct authors.
- Count the points.
- Announce the winning team.

---

### Step 5 – Reflection *(5 minutes)*

Facilitate a group discussion using questions such as:

- Which facts were the hardest to match?
- What surprised you about your classmates?
- Did you discover something about someone that you would never have learned through social media?
- How does technology influence what we choose to share about ourselves?
- How can authentic self-expression improve our mental wellbeing?

# 🎯 Objectives

This activity encourages participants to explore **identity beyond digital profiles and first impressions**.

Through creative writing, participants use **artistic self-expression** to communicate meaningful aspects of themselves that are often hidden in online spaces. Rather than focusing on appearance or social media identities, the activity highlights personal stories, experiences, values, and aspirations.

The activity addresses **mental wellbeing** by exploring how technology and social media often encourage curated or incomplete versions of ourselves, which can contribute to comparison, loneliness, and feelings of disconnection.

By shifting attention toward authentic, face-to-face interaction, participants:

- Build empathy.
- Strengthen trust within the group.
- Appreciate the complexity of each person's identity.
- Reflect on the difference between online personas and real-life relationships.
- Recognize how genuine conversations can foster healthier relationships with technology and support mental wellbeing.

---

# 💡 Learning Outcomes

After completing this activity, participants will be able to:

- Reflect on how technology influences personal identity and self-expression.
- Appreciate authentic storytelling as a form of creative expression.
- Build stronger interpersonal connections through empathy and active listening.
- Challenge assumptions formed through digital profiles and first impressions.
- Recognize the importance of genuine human interaction for supporting mental wellbeing.
