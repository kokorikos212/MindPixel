# Befriending Shame

> **Created by:** Jēkabs, Kamilla, Sofia, Nader

| **Activity Details** | |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Duration** | 30 minutes |
| **Participants** | 10–25 |
| **Location** | Outdoor (preferably in a quiet forest or natural setting) |
| **Facilitators** | 2–4 |
| **Recommended Environment** | Calm, safe, and distraction-free natural environment |

---

# 🎯 Topic Connection

This activity explores **mental wellbeing in the digital age** by encouraging participants to slow down, disconnect from technology, and reconnect with their emotions through **mindfulness and artistic self-expression**.

Technology and social media often encourage people to hide vulnerability or present only positive versions of themselves. By acknowledging and creatively expressing feelings of shame, participants learn that difficult emotions are a natural part of being human and that accepting them can support emotional wellbeing.

---

# 🧰 Materials Needed

- White paper
- Pens or pencils
- Sticky notes (Post-it notes)

---

# 📖 Activity Description

### Step 1 – Guided Meditation *(7 minutes)*

Gather participants in a quiet natural environment, preferably a forest.

Invite participants to remove their shoes (if they feel comfortable) and stand or sit barefoot on the ground.

Lead a guided meditation that encourages participants to:

- Become aware of their breathing.
- Notice the sounds, smells, and sensations of nature.
- Observe how they are feeling in the present moment.
- Create a sense of safety and openness before beginning the activity.

---

### Step 2 – Writing Reflection *(5 minutes)*

After the meditation, invite participants to quietly reflect on moments or experiences that have made them feel shame.

Provide prompts such as:

- What is something you have been ashamed of?
- How has this feeling affected you?
- What emotions come up when you think about it?
- What would you like to say to this part of yourself?

Participants write their reflections privately.

They are reminded that **they do not have to share anything they are uncomfortable sharing.**

---

### Step 3 – Creative Expression *(10 minutes)*

Participants are invited to transform their feelings of shame into an artistic expression using whatever is available around them.

They may choose to:

- Create symbols or shapes with natural materials.
- Draw or write.
- Arrange leaves, stones, branches, or other objects.
- Express themselves in any creative form that feels meaningful.

There is no right or wrong outcome—the purpose is simply to acknowledge and express emotions in a safe, non-judgmental way.

---

### Step 4 – Closing Meditation & Personal Commitment *(8 minutes)*

Guide participants through a second meditation focused on acceptance and self-compassion.

Invite them to reflect on questions such as:

- What strengths have helped you through difficult moments?
- What part of yourself are you proud of today?
- What would you like to forgive yourself for?
- What small step can you take to be kinder to yourself?

Finally, participants make a personal promise to themselves.

They choose a future date to revisit that promise and set a reminder in their phone's calendar so they can reflect on their progress later.

# 🎯 Objectives

This activity helps participants recognize, accept, and express feelings of shame through **mindfulness and artistic expression**.

By using creative expression as a tool, participants learn to explore difficult emotions in a safe and supportive environment rather than suppressing or hiding them.

The activity connects to the theme of **technology and mental wellbeing** by highlighting how digital spaces often encourage perfectionism, comparison, and the concealment of vulnerability. Taking time away from screens and engaging with nature allows participants to reconnect with themselves in a more authentic way.

Participants are encouraged to practice self-compassion, embrace vulnerability, and develop healthier emotional awareness that supports long-term mental wellbeing.

---

# 💡 Learning Outcomes

After completing this activity, participants will be able to:

- Recognize and acknowledge feelings of shame without judgment.
- Use artistic expression as a healthy outlet for difficult emotions.
- Practice mindfulness and emotional self-awareness.
- Reflect on the influence of technology and social media on self-image and vulnerability.
- Develop greater self-compassion and make a personal commitment to support their own mental wellbeing.
