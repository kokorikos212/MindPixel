# Guess Who?

> **Created by:** Milica, Elizaveta, Panagiotis, Raphaela, Robert

| **Activity Details** | |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Duration** | 30 minutes |
| **Participants** | 10–25 |
| **Location** | Indoor or outdoor |
| **Facilitators** | 2–5 |
| **Recommended Environment** | Calm environment suitable for writing and discussion |

---

# 🎯 Topic Connection

This activity explores the relationship between **technology, artificial intelligence, identity, and mental wellbeing**.

Participants creatively express who they believe they are through writing and then compare this with anonymous AI-generated profiles based solely on publicly available online information. By contrasting self-expression with algorithmic interpretation, the activity encourages reflection on digital footprints, privacy, authenticity, and the emotional impact of being represented by technology.

Art is used through **creative writing and storytelling**, allowing participants to communicate aspects of their identity beyond what AI or social media can infer.

---

# 🧰 Materials Needed

- Blank paper
- Pens
- Printed AI-generated descriptions (prepared in advance)

---

# 📍 Preparation

Before the activity:

1. Collect publicly available social media profiles for participants (preferably via profile links or screenshots).
2. Use an AI tool to generate anonymous psychological-style profiles based **only** on publicly visible information.
3. Remove all names and direct identifiers.
4. Print each AI-generated profile on a separate sheet of paper.

### Suggested AI Prompt

> Read the provided Instagram profile (name, username, bio, posts, location tags, follower/following counts, and interactions). Based only on available information, write a short, personal-style psychological portrait of the user in 2–4 sentences that feels like you are talking in the first person (using "I am"). The tone should be empathetic, observational, and grounded in everyday life, without mentioning visual details of the profile.
>
> Afterwards, add 1–2 sentences explaining what information can realistically be inferred from the profile (such as routines, travel patterns, interests, or social connections), what privacy risks this creates, and why this information may be valuable for profiling, manipulation, harassment, or data collection. Keep this section factual without inventing personal information.

# 📖 Activity Description

## Step 1 – Introduction *(5 minutes)*

Gather everyone together.

Explain:

- The purpose of the activity.
- That it explores identity, creativity, AI, and digital footprints.
- That the goal is reflection rather than competition.
- That all AI descriptions have been anonymised.

---

## Step 2 – Creative Self-Description *(5 minutes)*

Give each participant:

- One blank sheet of paper.
- One pen.

Participants write a short poetic or creative description of themselves.

Encourage them **not** to include:

- Their name
- Country of origin
- Physical appearance
- Any obvious identifying details

Instead, encourage descriptions based on personality, values, dreams, habits, interests, or life experiences.

### Example

> *I am a person in my mid-twenties living in a medium-sized city, balancing work, studies, and personal projects while always searching for reasons to travel. I believe the best version of myself appears whenever I step outside my comfort zone and explore somewhere new.*

---

## Step 3 – Divide into Two Groups *(3 minutes)*

Split participants evenly into:

- **Group A**
- **Group B**

Collect the handwritten descriptions separately so that the two groups' papers do not become mixed.

---

## Step 4 – Guessing Game *(10 minutes)*

### Round One

- **Group A** receives the anonymised AI-generated profiles of participants from **Group B**.
- Together they discuss which profile belongs to which participant.

At the same time:

- **Group B** receives the handwritten self-descriptions from **Group A**.
- They discuss and guess who wrote each description.

Participants record their guesses.

---

### Round Two

Swap the materials:

- Group A now receives the handwritten descriptions.
- Group B receives the AI-generated profiles.

Repeat the guessing activity.

---

## Step 5 – Reflection *(7 minutes)*

Bring everyone back together for a facilitated discussion.

Possible questions include:

- How did you feel during the activity?
- Was it easy or difficult to identify people?
- Which descriptions felt more authentic—the handwritten ones or the AI-generated ones? Why?
- How did you feel knowing the AI descriptions were created using only publicly available information?
- Did this activity change the way you think about your digital footprint?
- What can we do to manage our online presence more consciously?

# 🎯 Objectives

This activity aims to:

- Raise awareness about how much personal information can be inferred from publicly available online content.
- Encourage reflection on the capabilities and limitations of artificial intelligence.
- Compare self-perception with AI-generated interpretations.
- Explore the relationship between identity, authenticity, and online self-presentation.
- Promote conscious digital citizenship and safer online behaviour.
- Use creative writing as an artistic method for expressing identity beyond digital profiles.

---

# 💬 Debriefing Questions

- How did you feel during the activity?
- Was it easy or difficult to identify people from the descriptions?
- Which descriptions felt more authentic—the handwritten ones or the AI-generated ones?
- Were you surprised by what AI could infer using only public information?
- Did the activity change how you think about your digital footprint?
- What information do you intentionally or unintentionally reveal online?
- How can we protect our privacy while still expressing ourselves authentically?

---

# 💡 Learning Outcomes

After completing this activity, participants will be able to:

- Recognize how artificial intelligence can interpret publicly available personal information.
- Reflect on the differences between self-expression and algorithmic profiling.
- Better understand digital footprints and online privacy risks.
- Appreciate creative writing as a tool for expressing identity.
- Develop greater awareness of responsible technology use and its impact on mental wellbeing.
