# Music into Emotion Drawings

[[Jēkabs]] guided an exercise combining visual, spatial and auditory expression. With five blank sheets, markers, a list of six basic emotions and a playlist of five very different songs, we explored how music evokes feelings — and translated those emotions into abstract drawings. We loved seeing how differently each person interpreted the same music and emotions, celebrating individual perspectives.

**Playlist:** "Emotions" — playlist by Jēkabs Silanžs on Spotify (+ 5th song)

> [!quote] [[Jēkabs]] — insight about the workshop
> Creating my workshop was easy and exciting because it was inspired by several activities from last year's training course ([[MindSketch]]), as well as personal experiences that inspired the song choices and the feeling of nostalgia — I had a strong emotional connection to what I had on my mind.
>
> Last year a similar activity about visualising one's emotions truly opened up my creativity and helped me to process and discern my emotions and feelings. It kickstarted the journey of unlocking my potential for conceptualising and creating something that might be considered art. I wanted to share this experience with this year's participants, so it would be easier for them to connect with their emotions, put them in words and visualizations, in order to take their next step towards opening up and creating something they might be proud about, as I have been quite a few times since the last year's project.
>
> P.S. I have used this activity with my teenage students before reading and writing poetry to better understand abstract characters and thoughts. Thus, there are many ways of applying and adjusting it.
